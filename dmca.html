DMCA & Copyright Policy

Last Updated: June 2026

Radio Rainbow respects the intellectual property rights of music artists, sound engineers, labels, and content developers. In accordance with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA), we have established a clear system for responding to incoming claims of copyright infringement.

Filing an Infringement Takedown Notice

If you are a copyright administrator or designated legal representative, and you assert that content hosted on our promotional news logs, images, or tracking catalogues infringes upon your ownership acts, you may submit a formal notification by emailing our department directly at:

radiorainbow15@gmail.com

Your written DMCA Notice must include the following verification details:

  • An electronic or physical signature of the person authorized to act on behalf of the owner of the copyright interest.
  • A clear description identifying the copyrighted work, song, image, or media link that you claim has been breached.
  • The direct URL or specific area on our station application where the disputed material is located.
  • Your direct contact routing info, including your physical mailing address, working telephone number, and active email address.
  • A statement by you, made under penalty of perjury, that the information in your notice is accurate and that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on the copyright owner's behalf.

Audio Streams Disclaimer

Please note that the live audio streaming parameters are generated and delivered externally via embedded third-party widgets (Zeno.FM). Radio Rainbow does not store audio files or host music tracks on its root server directory. If your copyright issue relates directly to the playing song file on the live stream itself, you may also need to file your notice directly with the source streaming distribution company to achieve a global track removal.
